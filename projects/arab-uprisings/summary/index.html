Social Media and the Arab Uprisings

Research Summary

This final research project looks at the relationship between social media and the revolutions of the Arab uprisings in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya. It will first look at the history of these countries and the published analyses of social media's role in the movements. Then, the current role of social media and the status of the governments' democratic efforts will be reviewed.

The review of Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya's histories and the assessment of their present-day situations will be used to better understand what makes a revolutionary movement—particularly one involving social media—take hold more effectively in some places than in others.

Research Outline

This is the syllabus. The sections of the project are defined by a topic and list of questions which will direct the focus of research.

  1. Background of the revolutionary movements in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya as parts of the Arab uprisings
    • What triggered the movements in these countries?
    • What were the goals of the revolutionaries?
    • Did they attain them? If so, how?
  2. Analyses of social media's involvement in the revolutionary movements in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya
    • What kinds of opinions are there on the role of social media in the Arab uprisings?
    • Do they differ by geography (e.g. The West vs. North African/Middle Eastern countries), religion, or political stance?
  3. The role of social media in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya today
    • Does social media use look similar or different?
    • How and why?
  4. The status and stability of governmental institutions in Tunisia, Egypt, and Libya today
    • Did the revolutionary parties achieve their goals?
    • Are they still in power?
    • What has changed?
    • What is the same?
  5. Conclusion
    • Did technology empower or weaken the revolutionary movements of the Arab uprisings in the long run?
    • What other factors played a role in these countries' successes or failures?
    • How have the revolutionary movements of the Arab uprisings impacted other areas of the world?
    • How will social media influence future movements?
    • What makes a revolutionary movement—particularly one involving social media—take hold more effectively in some places than in others?

Blog Posts

I'll write 750-word posts reflecting specifically on each of the countries for the four topics in my research outline. I will then complete the project with a reflective post covering the questions outlined in my conclusion. Ultimately, though, the blog is my final product.

This project considers the role of social media in society, so I’ll share these blog posts with my peers via the GTD Facebook group and on Twitter in an attempt to elicit feedback from classmates, faculty in the GTD program, and my peers. The benefit of this approach is that the conversation on this topic has the opportunity continue to grow and/or evolve even after the research project is finished.
